⚠️ Venetoclax Dosing with Azoles - CRITICAL

With posaconazole or voriconazole: Reduce venetoclax to 100 mg daily (75% reduction)

With fluconazole: Reduce venetoclax to 200 mg daily (50% reduction)

TLS risk: Very high in first cycle, especially with high WBC or bulky disease

📋 Resident Action Items - Cycle 1 Critical

  • Day 0: Check BMP, uric acid, LDH, phosphorus. Start allopurinol 300mg daily OR rasburicase if high risk
  • Day 1: Aggressive hydration (150 mL/hr), venetoclax ramp (100mg), monitor BMP/UA/LDH q6-8h
  • Days 2-3: Continue labs q12-24h during venetoclax ramp
  • Days 7-14: Expect cytopenias, monitor CBC 2-3x/week
  • Days 21-28: Assess response (BM biopsy if counts recovering or Day 28)
  • Infection prophylaxis: Start posaconazole (adjust venetoclax!), acyclovir, atovaquone
Tumor Lysis Syndrome

Highest risk: Cycle 1, especially if WBC >25K

Monitor: BMP, uric acid, phosphorus, LDH q6-12h × 2-3 days

Treatment: Rasburicase, aggressive hydration, hold venetoclax if severe

Myelosuppression

Expected: Prolonged cytopenias cycle 1-2

Action: May need to delay subsequent cycles, dose-reduce venetoclax to 7-14 days

Infections

Risk: Very high due to prolonged neutropenia

Prophylaxis: Antifungal (azole), antiviral (acyclovir), PJP (atovaquone)

GI Toxicity

Common: Nausea, diarrhea (especially azacitidine)

Management: Aggressive antiemetics, loperamide prn

⚠️ Cerebellar Toxicity - WATCH CLOSELY

Risk factors: Age >60, renal dysfunction, cumulative dose, q12h dosing

Symptoms: Ataxia, dysarthria, nystagmus, difficulty with fine motor tasks

Action: STOP HiDAC immediately if any cerebellar signs. Toxicity may be irreversible!

📋 Resident Action Items

  • Before each dose: Neuro exam (finger-to-nose, gait, handwriting test)
  • Pre-treatment: Check CrCl, reduce dose if <60 mL/min
  • Eye care: Steroid eye drops (prednisolone 1%) BID during treatment to prevent conjunctivitis
  • Hydration: 150 mL/hr NS before and after each dose
  • Nadir: Expect Day 14-21, monitor CBC 2-3x/week
  • G-CSF: Start Day 7, continue until ANC >1000

⚠️ Differentiation Syndrome with IDH/FLT3 Inhibitors

Incidence: 10-20% with IDH inhibitors, gilteritinib

Timing: Usually within first month, can occur anytime

Symptoms: Fever, dyspnea, hypoxia, pulmonary infiltrates, pleural/pericardial effusions, peripheral edema, hypotension, AKI

Treatment: Dexamethasone 10 mg IV BID, hold drug if severe, diuretics if volume overloaded

Acute Promyelocytic Leukemia (APL)

⚠️ APL is a MEDICAL EMERGENCY

Start ATRA immediately if APL suspected - do NOT wait for confirmatory testing!

Early mortality is from bleeding (DIC) - start treatment ASAP

Diagnostic Features

  • Morphology: Promyelocytes with Auer rods (faggot cells)
  • Flow: CD34 negative, CD33+, HLA-DR negative
  • Genetic: PML-RARA fusion (t(15;17))
  • Coags: Low fibrinogen, elevated D-dimer, prolonged PT/PTT

Induction: Continue until CR (usually 30-60 days), confirm with bone marrow showing <5% blasts

Consolidation:

  • ATO: 0.15 mg/kg IV 5 days/week for 4 weeks, every 8 weeks × 4 cycles
  • ATRA: 45 mg/m² PO BID for 2 weeks, every 4 weeks × 7 cycles

📋 Daily Resident Checklist for APL

  • Coags EVERY morning: PT, PTT, fibrinogen, D-dimer (goal fibrinogen >150)
  • Platelet transfusion: Keep >30-50K at ALL times (even higher if active bleeding)
  • Cryoprecipitate: If fibrinogen <100-150 mg/dL, give 10 units
  • Monitor WBC: If rising rapidly, may need hydroxyurea or chemotherapy
  • Watch for differentiation syndrome: Daily weights, O2 sat, exam for edema/crackles
  • ECG: Baseline, then 2x/week (ATO causes QTc prolongation)
  • Electrolytes: Daily (ATO can cause hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia)

⚠️ CRITICAL - Coagulopathy Management

Goal fibrinogen >150 mg/dL - transfuse cryoprecipitate liberally

Goal platelets >30-50K - may need multiple units daily

DO NOT give FFP routinely - only if active bleeding and elevated PT/PTT

Avoid procedures until coags stabilize (typically 5-7 days after starting ATRA)

NO ANTICOAGULATION unless absolutely necessary

⚠️ Differentiation Syndrome - LIFE THREATENING

Timing: Usually Days 2-21 (median Day 10-12)

Clinical features:

  • Fever (often first sign)
  • Dyspnea, hypoxia
  • Pulmonary infiltrates on CXR
  • Pleural or pericardial effusions
  • Peripheral edema, weight gain (>5 kg)
  • Hypotension
  • Acute kidney injury

Treatment - START IMMEDIATELY if suspected:

  • Dexamethasone 10 mg IV BID (continue for at least 3 days after resolution, taper over 1-2 weeks)
  • Hold ATRA/ATO if severe (can restart after resolution)
  • Diuretics if volume overloaded
  • Supportive care: O2, monitoring in ICU if severe
QTc Prolongation (ATO)

Incidence: Very common (>50%)

Monitor: ECG 2-3x/week

Action: Hold ATO if QTc >500ms, replete K >4, Mg >2

Headache (ATRA)

Incidence: Very common (>50%)

Management: Acetaminophen, divide dose to TID/QID, rule out pseudotumor cerebri if severe

Hepatotoxicity

Both ATRA and ATO can cause transaminitis

Monitor: LFTs weekly

Action: Hold if AST/ALT >5x ULN

Peripheral Neuropathy (ATO)

Usually in consolidation

Monitor: Symptoms, exam

Action: Dose reduce or hold ATO

📋 Hyper-CVAD Monitoring

Part A:

  • Check urine for blood q8h during cyclophosphamide (hemorrhagic cystitis risk)
  • Aggressive hydration (150-200 mL/hr)
  • Constipation prophylaxis for vincristine

Part B:

  • MTX levels: Check at 24h (end of infusion), then daily until <0.1 μmol/L
  • Increase leucovorin if MTX level elevated (50 mg IV q6h if level >5 at 48h)
  • Alkalinize urine (bicarbonate drip to keep pH >7)
  • Daily BMP, Mg, Phos during MTX infusion
  • Neuro checks before each cytarabine dose (cerebellar toxicity)

CNS Prophylaxis & Treatment

NO CNS involvement (CNS-1):

  • Cycle 1: IT MTX or cytarabine on Days 1, 14
  • Cycle 2: IT MTX or cytarabine on Days 1, 8, 15, 22
  • Dose: MTX 12 mg IT OR cytarabine 50-70 mg IT
  • Route: Lumbar puncture or Ommaya reservoir

⚠️ CNS Involvement Present (CNS-2 or CNS-3)

IT chemotherapy: Weekly during induction (minimum 4 doses), then every 2 weeks during consolidation

Total doses: Minimum 8-12 doses, continue until 2 consecutive clear LPs

Consider: Triple IT therapy (MTX + cytarabine + hydrocortisone) for CNS-3

Radiation: May need cranial RT if refractory CNS disease

📋 IT Chemotherapy Administration

  • Timing: Give BEFORE systemic chemotherapy when possible
  • Platelet goal: >50K (some say >20K acceptable if no other bleeding)
  • Technique: Remove equivalent volume of CSF before injecting drug
  • Post-procedure: Lie flat × 30-60 minutes to prevent headache
  • Send CSF: Cell count, cytology, flow cytometry (if CNS+)

ALL-Specific Toxicities

Vincristine Neuropathy

Symptoms: Paresthesias, constipation, foot drop, jaw pain

Prevention: Bowel regimen (senna, miralax)

Management: Dose reduce if severe, gabapentin for pain

Asparaginase Reactions

Allergic: Rash, anaphylaxis (more with E. coli formulation)

Pancreatitis: Check amylase/lipase before each dose, hold if >3x ULN

Thrombosis: Check fibrinogen weekly, give FFP if <100

Hepatotoxicity: Monitor LFTs, usually transient

Steroid Side Effects

Common: Hyperglycemia, insomnia, mood changes, proximal myopathy

Monitoring: Fingersticks QID, PPI for GI prophylaxis

PJP prophylaxis: Atovaquone or Bactrim while on steroids

MTX Toxicity

Mucositis, renal dysfunction, hepatotoxicity

Prevention: Alkalinize urine, aggressive hydration

Monitor: MTX levels, increase leucovorin if delayed clearance

Common Toxicities & Management

Febrile Neutropenia

⚠️ Definition & Initial Management

Definition: ANC <500 AND single temp >38.3°C OR temp >38°C for >1 hour

IMMEDIATE actions (within 1 hour):

  • Blood cultures × 2 (peripheral and from each lumen of central line)
  • Urine culture, CXR
  • CBC with differential, BMP, LFTs
  • Start empiric antibiotics (don't wait for culture results!)

Empiric Antibiotic Regimens

First-line (choose one):

  • Cefepime 2g IV q8h
  • Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5g IV q6h
  • Meropenem 1g IV q8h (if prior resistant organisms or severe sepsis)

Add vancomycin if:

  • Hemodynamically unstable
  • Skin/soft tissue infection
  • Mucositis
  • Positive blood culture for Gram-positive cocci before speciation
  • Prior MRSA colonization/infection

Consider antifungal if:

  • Persistent fever >4-7 days despite broad-spectrum antibiotics
  • CT chest showing nodules/infiltrates
  • Galactomannan or beta-D-glucan positive
  • Options: Micafungin 100mg IV daily, voriconazole 6mg/kg IV q12h × 2 doses then 4mg/kg q12h, liposomal amphotericin B 3-5mg/kg IV daily

Diarrhea

⚠️ Rule Out C. difficile in Neutropenic Patients

Send stool for C. diff PCR if diarrhea, especially if recent antibiotics

Treatment if positive: Vancomycin 125mg PO QID × 10-14 days (first-line), fidaxomicin if recurrent

Tumor Lysis Syndrome (TLS)

⚠️ Laboratory TLS Criteria

  • Uric acid >8 mg/dL or 25% increase
  • Potassium >6 mEq/L or 25% increase
  • Phosphorus >4.5 mg/dL or 25% increase
  • Calcium <7 mg/dL or 25% decrease

⚠️ Azole-Venetoclax Interaction - CRITICAL

If patient on venetoclax + azole antifungal:

  • Posaconazole or voriconazole: Reduce venetoclax to 100mg daily (75% reduction)
  • Fluconazole: Reduce venetoclax to 200mg daily (50% reduction)

Pre-Treatment Checklist

✓ Before Starting Chemotherapy

  • Cardiac: ECHO or MUGA (baseline EF)
  • Infectious disease: HIV, HBV, HCV serologies
  • Tumor lysis labs: BMP, uric acid, LDH, phosphorus
  • Coagulation: PT, PTT, fibrinogen, D-dimer (especially APL)
  • Type & screen: For transfusion support
  • Pregnancy test: In all patients of childbearing potential
  • Fertility preservation: Discuss and offer referral BEFORE chemotherapy
  • Central line: Tunneled catheter (Hickman) or PICC for intensive therapy
  • Dental clearance: Especially before intensive chemotherapy or transplant
  • Advance directives: Goals of care discussion
  • Social work: Financial counseling, disability paperwork

When to Consult Transplant

Consult BMT team early for:

  • All AML patients achieving CR1 (especially intermediate/high-risk cytogenetics)
  • ALL patients in CR1 (most will need transplant)
  • Relapsed/refractory disease considering salvage therapy
  • High-risk molecular features (FLT3-ITD with high allelic ratio, TP53 mutation, complex karyotype)

Do NOT delay: HLA typing can take 2-4 weeks, donor search can take months
