💉 Prophylactic Dexamethasone

Evidence Base — ZUMA-1 Trial: Dexamethasone 10mg given on Day 0 through Day +2 after axi-cel CAR-T. Studies show fewer CRS/ICANS events without a decrease in treatment response.
Georgetown Protocol — Prophylactic Dexamethasone Institutional Protocol
Timing Dose Notes
Day 0 through Day +7 (or discharge, whichever is sooner) Dexamethasone 10mg Inpatient prophylaxis
At discharge Dexamethasone 10mg × 1 dose Given in case patient develops fever at home after discharge

✓ Key Points

  • Does not decrease treatment response to CAR-T
  • Reduces incidence of CRS and ICANS
  • One discharge dose provided as a safety net for post-discharge fever

Cytopenias

🩸 Post CAR-T Cytopenias

Cytopenias can occur in two patterns after CAR-T therapy:

  • Immediate: Shortly after CAR-T infusion, as expected from lymphodepletion
  • Delayed: A more prolonged cytopenias that can develop weeks to months after therapy

See reference link #1 below for a detailed approach to post CAR-T cytopenias.

💊 Antibiotic Prophylaxis

Category Agent(s) Timing / Notes
Antiviral Acyclovir 400mg BID Valacyclovir 500mg QD All patients — start at time of CAR-T
PJP Prophylaxis Bactrim DS MWF Atovaquone 1500mg daily Starts at Day 30 post CAR-T infusion
Antibacterial Levofloxacin 500mg daily Neutropenic patients only
Antifungal Fluconazole 400mg daily Posaconazole 300mg daily Neutropenic patients only
Note: Antibacterial and antifungal prophylaxis are indicated only in neutropenic patients. Antiviral prophylaxis and PJP prophylaxis (starting Day 30) are given to all patients regardless of counts.
