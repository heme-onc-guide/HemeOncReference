Do not manage CRS or ICANS overnight without notifying the team. Early escalation is critical.

Treatments That Can Cause CRS / ICANS

🔬 CAR-T Cell Therapy

Chimeric antigen receptor T-cell therapy — highest risk for both CRS and ICANS

💉 Bispecific T-Cell Engagers (BiTE)
  • Talquetamab — for multiple myeloma
  • Epcoritamab — for lymphoma

ICE Score (Immune effector Cell-associated Encephalopathy) — 10-point bedside assessment: orientation to year/month/city/hospital (4 pts), naming 3 objects (3 pts), following commands (1 pt), writing a sentence (1 pt), counting backward from 100 by 10 (1 pt).

🔥 CRS Treatment

⚠️ Any Suspicion of CRS → Call Attending / Fellow First

Do not manage CRS independently overnight. Always escalate before initiating tocilizumab or steroids.

Reference: Management of Immune-Related Adverse Events in Patients Treated With CAR-T Cell Therapy: ASCO Guideline
📖 ASCO Guideline: JCO.21.01992
Grade 1
Grade 1
Fever only — no hypotension, no hypoxia
  • Supportive care: Tylenol, IV fluids
  • Antibiotics if neutropenic fever
Grade 2
Grade 2
Fever + hypotension not requiring vasopressor, or hypoxia requiring nasal cannula only
  • Supportive care: Tylenol, IV fluids
  • Tocilizumab 8 mg/kg IV — repeat every 8h up to 3 doses in 24h (4 doses total)
  • If hypotension refractory to fluids + tocilizumab → add Dexamethasone 10mg IV q12h
Grade 3
Grade 3
Fever + hypotension requiring vasopressor, or hypoxia requiring NRB or high-flow O₂
  • Supportive care
  • ICU admission for vasopressor management
  • ECHO
  • Tocilizumab 8 mg/kg IV — repeat every 8h up to 3 doses in 24h (4 doses total)
  • Dexamethasone 10mg IV q6h
Grade 4
Grade 4
Fever + hypotension requiring multiple vasopressors, or hypoxia requiring BiPAP or mechanical ventilation
  • Supportive care
  • ICU admission for vasopressors / mechanical ventilation
  • Tocilizumab 8 mg/kg IV — repeat every 8h up to 3 doses in 24h (4 doses total)
  • Methylprednisolone 500mg IV q12h

Tocilizumab Quick Reference

Parameter Details
Dose 8 mg/kg IV
Repeat dosing Every 8 hours — up to 3 doses in 24 hours
Maximum total doses 4 doses total
Escalation If refractory: add dexamethasone (Grade 2) or methylprednisolone (Grade 4)

🧠 ICANS Treatment

🧠 ICANS Key Principles

  • Always assess for concurrent CRS — this changes management significantly
  • Aspiration precautions in all ICANS grades
  • Tocilizumab is used for concurrent CRS; dexamethasone/methylprednisolone target ICANS directly
Grade 1
ICE 7–9
Fatigued but awakens spontaneously
No Concurrent CRS:
  • Supportive care, IV fluids
  • Aspiration precautions
Concurrent CRS:
  • Tocilizumab (per CRS grade)
  • Consider IV Dex after first tocilizumab dose
Grade 2
ICE 3–6
Awakens to voice
No Concurrent CRS:
  • Dexamethasone 10mg IV q6–12h until resolution
Concurrent CRS:
  • Tocilizumab (per CRS grade)
  • Consider ICU admission
  • Consider IV Dex after first tocilizumab dose
Grade 3
ICE 0–2
Awakens only to tactile stimulus; mild/subclinical seizure; focal cerebral edema
  • ICU admission
No Concurrent CRS:
  • Dexamethasone 10mg IV q6–12h until resolution
Concurrent CRS:
  • Tocilizumab (per CRS grade)
  • Consider IV Dex after first tocilizumab dose
Grade 4
ICE 0
Unarousable; severe/prolonged seizure; diffuse cerebral edema
  • ICU admission
No Concurrent CRS:
  • Methylprednisolone 1g IV 1–2× daily for 3 days
  • Can increase frequency if refractory
Concurrent CRS:
  • Tocilizumab (per CRS grade)
  • Methylprednisolone 1g IV 1–2× daily for 3 days
  • Can increase frequency if refractory
