🩺 Acute Leukemia Treatment Guide

Comprehensive reference for AML, APL, and ALL treatment protocols with dosing, toxicities, and monitoring

Due to size constraints, the full acute leukemia content has been condensed. Please refer to the standalone acute leukemia guide for complete details including all regimens, day-by-day charts, and comprehensive monitoring protocols.

Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML)

Treatment pathway: Induction → Consolidation → Allogeneic transplant (if eligible)

Key Regimens

  • 7+3: Cytarabine 100-200 mg/m² continuous infusion Days 1-7 + Anthracycline Days 1-3
  • Vyxeos (CPX-351): For treatment-related AML or prior MDS
  • FLAVIDA: Fludarabine + Cytarabine + G-CSF + Idarubicin + Venetoclax
  • HMA-Venetoclax: For older/unfit patients

Acute Promyelocytic Leukemia (APL)

⚠️ APL is a MEDICAL EMERGENCY

Start ATRA immediately if suspected - do NOT wait for confirmatory testing!

Standard treatment: ATRA + ATO (add anthracycline if high-risk WBC >10K)

Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL)

Key regimens: E1910, Hyper-CVAD

Special considerations: BCR-ABL+ (add TKI), CD20+ (add Rituximab), CNS prophylaxis

🔬 Multiple Myeloma Treatment Guide

Comprehensive resident reference for MM diagnosis, treatment protocols, and monitoring

Diagnosis & Workup

"Myeloma Labs" - Initial Workup

✓ Laboratory Studies to Order

  • SPEP (Serum Protein Electrophoresis)
  • UPEP (Urine Protein Electrophoresis)
  • Serum free light chains (Kappa/Lambda with ratio)
  • Quantitative immunoglobulins (IgG, IgA, IgM)
  • Immunofixation (serum and urine)
  • CBC, CMP, LDH, beta-2 microglobulin, albumin
  • Bone marrow biopsy with flow, cytogenetics, FISH

SLIM-CRAB Diagnostic Criteria

Requires: Clonal BM plasma cells ≥10% PLUS one or more:

CRAB (End-Organ Damage)

  • C: Calcium >11 mg/dL
  • R: Renal impairment (CrCl <40 or Cr >2)
  • A: Anemia (Hgb <10 g/dL)
  • B: Bone lesions (≥1 on imaging)

SLiM (Biomarkers)

  • S: ≥60% clonal plasma cells in BM
  • Li: Free light chain ratio ≥100
  • M: >1 focal lesion on MRI (≥5mm)

Risk Stratification

⚠️ High-Risk Cytogenetics

Translocations: t(4;14), t(14;16), t(14;20)

Deletions: del(17p) - TP53, del(1p)

Gain: gain(1q) or amp(1q)

ISS/R-ISS Staging: Based on β2M, albumin, LDH, and cytogenetics

First-Line Therapy

D-VRd (Quadruplet Induction) First-Line
Indication: Newly diagnosed MM, transplant-eligible

Components:

  • Daratumumab (D): 16 mg/kg SQ weekly × 8, then q2 weeks
  • Bortezomib (V): 1.3 mg/m² IV/SQ Days 1, 4, 8, 11
  • Lenalidomide (R): 25 mg PO Days 1-21
  • Dexamethasone (d): 40 mg PO/IV weekly

Cycle: 21 or 28 days × 4-6 cycles pre-transplant

📋 Key Monitoring

  • Daratumumab: Pre-med for infusion reactions
  • Bortezomib: Check for neuropathy, SQ preferred
  • Lenalidomide: VTE prophylaxis (aspirin 81mg or LMWH)
  • Antimicrobial prophylaxis: Acyclovir, Bactrim/atovaquone

Maintenance

Standard: Lenalidomide 10-15 mg PO daily (Days 1-21) until progression

High-risk: Consider adding proteasome inhibitor (bortezomib or carfilzomib)

Relapsed/Refractory Disease

Common regimens:

  • DPd: Daratumumab + Pomalidomide + Dex
  • DKd: Daratumumab + Carfilzomib + Dex
  • XPd: Selinexor + Pomalidomide + Dex
  • CyBorD: Cyclophosphamide + Bortezomib + Dex

Special: For t(11;14), consider venetoclax-based therapy

Bispecific T-Cell Engagers & CAR-T

Teclistamab (Tecvayli)

Mechanism: CD3 × BCMA bispecific

Dosing: Step-up 0.06 → 0.3 → 1.5 mg/kg (2-4 days apart), then 1.5 mg/kg weekly

⚠️ CRS/ICANS Risk

Peak risk: 24-72h after step-up doses

Monitor inpatient: 24-48h after first doses

Have tocilizumab available

CAR-T (Ide-cel, Cilta-cel)

Indication: Relapsed/refractory MM after 1-3 prior lines

Process: Leukapheresis → Manufacturing (3-4 weeks) → Lymphodepletion → Infusion → Monitoring
