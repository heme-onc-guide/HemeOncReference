Diagnosis & Workup

"Myeloma Labs" - Initial Workup

✓ Laboratory Studies to Order

  • SPEP (Serum Protein Electrophoresis)
  • UPEP (Urine Protein Electrophoresis) - does NOT need to be 24-hour collection
  • Serum free light chains (Kappa and Lambda) with ratio
  • Quantitative immunoglobulins (IgG, IgA, IgM)
  • Immunofixation (both serum and urine)
  • CBC with differential
  • CMP (including calcium, creatinine)
  • LDH, beta-2 microglobulin, albumin (for ISS/R-ISS staging)
  • Bone marrow biopsy with flow cytometry, cytogenetics, FISH

✓ Imaging Studies

  • Whole body low-dose CT OR PET/CT (preferred) OR MRI spine/pelvis
  • Looking for lytic lesions, fractures, focal lesions
  • Note: Plain skeletal survey is outdated - use CT/PET/MRI

Clonal bone marrow plasma cells ≥10%

PLUS one or more of the following:

High-Risk Cytogenetics

⚠️ High-Risk Features (Require Intensified Therapy)

Translocations:

  • t(4;14) - FGFR3/MMSET rearrangement
  • t(14;16) - MAF rearrangement
  • t(14;20) - MAFB rearrangement

Deletions:

  • del(17p) - TP53 deletion (worst prognosis)
  • del(1p)

Gain/Amplification:

  • gain(1q) or amp(1q)

📋 High-Risk Patient Management

  • Use quadruplet induction regimens
  • Proceed to autologous stem cell transplant in CR1 if eligible
  • Consider dual-agent maintenance (lenalidomide + proteasome inhibitor)
  • Earlier referral for allogeneic transplant or clinical trials
  • More frequent monitoring for relapse

First-Line Therapy

Treatment Algorithm for Transplant-Eligible Patients

Induction (3-6 cycles quadruplet) → Stem cell collection → Autologous SCT → Maintenance (lenalidomide ± PI)

📋 Resident Action Items

  • Daratumumab: Pre-med with acetaminophen, diphenhydramine, montelukast (or steroid). Post-med with steroid. Monitor for infusion reactions (first dose especially).
  • Bortezomib: SQ preferred over IV to reduce neuropathy risk. Hold/reduce dose if Grade 2+ neuropathy.
  • Lenalidomide: Dose adjust for CrCl <60. Requires REMS program enrollment. Give with aspirin 81mg daily for VTE prophylaxis (or LMWH if high risk).
  • Dexamethasone: Reduce to 20mg weekly in frail/elderly patients. PPI for GI prophylaxis. Monitor blood sugars.
  • Supportive care: PJP prophylaxis (Bactrim or atovaquone), acyclovir 400mg BID for HSV prophylaxis, calcium/vitamin D.

⚠️ Key Toxicities to Monitor

  • Peripheral neuropathy (bortezomib) - check before each dose, switch to weekly dosing if Grade 2+
  • Cytopenias (lenalidomide, daratumumab) - monitor CBC weekly initially
  • VTE risk (lenalidomide + dex) - use aspirin or LMWH prophylaxis
  • Infections (immunosuppression) - antimicrobial prophylaxis essential
  • Hyperglycemia (dexamethasone) - monitor glucose, may need insulin

Cycle length: 28 days

⚠️ Carfilzomib-Specific Monitoring

  • Cardiac toxicity: Baseline ECHO required. Monitor for HF, arrhythmias. Hold if EF drops significantly.
  • Hypertension: Very common (>50%). Monitor BP before each dose, treat with antihypertensives.
  • Renal toxicity: Hydrate pre/post infusion (250-500 mL NS). Monitor creatinine.
  • Infusion reactions: Pre-medicate with dexamethasone.

Transplant-Ineligible / Frail Patients

Dara-VRd or Isa-VRd (Reduced Intensity) First-Line
Indication: Frail, elderly, or transplant-ineligible newly diagnosed MM

Same regimen as above but with modifications:

  • Bortezomib: Weekly dosing (Days 1, 8, 15, 22) instead of twice weekly
  • Lenalidomide: 15-25 mg daily (dose adjust for age/CrCl)
  • Dexamethasone: 20 mg weekly (instead of 40 mg)
  • Isatuximab (Isa): Can substitute for daratumumab: 10 mg/kg IV weekly × 4, then every 2 weeks

Key trials: BENEFIT (Nature) and CEPHEUS (Nature) support quadruplet therapy even in transplant-ineligible patients

Standard approach: Continue indefinitely until progression or intolerance

📋 Maintenance Monitoring

  • CBC: Monthly initially, then every 3 months once stable
  • SPEP, free light chains: Every 2-3 months
  • CMP: Every 3 months
  • Continue VTE prophylaxis: Aspirin 81mg daily (or LMWH if high risk)
  • Antimicrobial prophylaxis: Can often discontinue after 3-6 months if no active issues
Dual-Agent Maintenance (High-Risk) High-Risk
Indication: High-risk cytogenetics post-transplant

Options:

  • Lenalidomide + Bortezomib: Lenalidomide 10-15 mg daily (D1-21) + Bortezomib 1.3 mg/m² SQ every 2 weeks
  • Lenalidomide + Carfilzomib: Lenalidomide 10-15 mg daily (D1-21) + Carfilzomib 56 mg/m² IV Days 1, 2, 15, 16

Rationale: High-risk patients benefit from continued proteasome inhibitor exposure

⚠️ Selinexor Side Effects

  • Nausea/vomiting: Very common (>70%). Aggressive antiemetics (olanzapine 5-10mg helpful). Give on full stomach.
  • Anorexia/weight loss: Nutritional support, appetite stimulants
  • Fatigue: Dose reductions common
  • Thrombocytopenia: Monitor platelets closely
  • Hyponatremia: Monitor sodium, usually mild

Special Populations

t(11;14) Translocation

Consider Venetoclax-based therapy:

  • Venetoclax 800 mg daily (with ramp-up) + Dexamethasone
  • Higher response rates in t(11;14) patients
  • TLS risk: Use TLS prophylaxis, ramp venetoclax slowly

Bispecific T-Cell Engagers & CAR-T

Treatment sequencing in relapsed MM:

  • 1-3 prior lines: Consider CAR-T (ide-cel, cilta-cel)
  • ≥4 prior lines: Consider bispecific antibodies

⚠️ CRS/ICANS Risk - HIGHEST after first full dose

Timing: Peak risk ~24-72 hours after 0.3 mg/kg and 1.5 mg/kg doses

Prevention:

  • Pre-medications: Acetaminophen, diphenhydramine
  • Monitor inpatient for 24-48h after first two step-up doses
  • Dexamethasone 16 mg prior to step-up doses in high-risk patients

Treatment: See CRS/ICANS section - tocilizumab, steroids

📋 Monitoring During Step-Up

  • Vital signs q4h for 24-48h after step-up doses
  • Continuous pulse oximetry
  • Neurological assessments q6-12h (ICE score)
  • Have tocilizumab readily available on unit
  • Low threshold for transfer to ICU if severe CRS/ICANS

CAR-T Cell Therapy

Ide-cel (Abecma) & Cilta-cel (Carvykti) CAR-T
Indication: Relapsed/refractory MM after 1-3 prior lines (depending on product)

Process:

  1. Leukapheresis: Collect T-cells
  2. Manufacturing: 3-4 weeks (patient gets bridging therapy)
  3. Lymphodepletion: Fludarabine + cyclophosphamide × 3 days
  4. CAR-T infusion: Single infusion
  5. Monitoring: Inpatient × 7-10 days minimum

⚠️ CAR-T Specific Toxicities

  • CRS: Usually Days 1-7, manage with tocilizumab ± steroids
  • ICANS: Usually Days 3-10, often delayed compared to CRS
  • Cytopenias: Prolonged, may need growth factors/transfusions
  • Infections: Profound immunosuppression, antimicrobial prophylaxis essential
  • Hypogammaglobulinemia: Monitor Igs, may need IVIG replacement

📋 Post-CAR-T Monitoring

  • Days 0-7: Inpatient, vitals q4h, neurological checks q8-12h
  • Days 7-30: Outpatient with close follow-up, must stay within 2 hours of CAR-T center
  • Labs: CBC, CMP, LFTs 2-3x/week initially
  • Antimicrobials: Continue prophylaxis for 6-12 months
  • IVIG: Check IgG monthly, replace if <400 mg/dL
