🌡️ Neutropenic Fever

Evidence-based workup, empiric antibiotic selection, fungal coverage, and antimicrobial prophylaxis in hematologic malignancies

🚨 Overnight Recommendations

⚠️ Suspected Neutropenic Fever — Act Immediately

  1. Send infectious workup: blood cultures (×2), CXR, urinalysis ± urine culture
  2. Assess patient for localizing infectious symptoms
  3. Start Cefepime empirically — do not delay antibiotics for workup
When to Add Vancomycin to Cefepime Escalate

Add vancomycin if any of the following are present:

Indication Rationale
Skin involvement / oral ulcers Gram+ skin flora / mucositis
Concern for line infection (CVC) Gram+ bacteremia (Staph, Strep)
Pneumonia (PNA) CAP organisms including Strep pneumo
Hemodynamic instability / severe sepsis Broad empiric coverage
Blood culture with gram+ bacteria Targeted coverage
Known MRSA colonization Empiric MRSA coverage

When is it OK to give Tylenol?
In a patient already receiving appropriate antibiotics for neutropenic fever, it is acceptable to give acetaminophen for symptomatic relief of fever. Do not use Tylenol to mask fever prior to completing the infectious workup.

Definition & Thresholds Definition
Category ANC Clinical Significance
Neutropenia <500–1000 cells/µL Increased infection risk
Severe / Prolonged Neutropenia <500 cells/µL for ≥7 days Antimicrobial prophylaxis indicated

⚠️ Prolonged Neutropenia (<500 for ≥7 days): Prophylaxis Indicated

In patients experiencing prolonged neutropenia, antimicrobial prophylaxis covering antibacterial, antifungal, and antiviral organisms is indicated. See the Prophylaxis section below for details.

Fungal Diagnostic Testing

🧪 Beta-D-Glucan (Fungitell)

Element in fungal cell wall. Positive in:

  • Candida
  • Aspergillus
  • PJP infections

Broad fungal screen — not specific

🧪 Galactomannan

Specific testing to identify Aspergillus infections only.

Order when Aspergillus specifically suspected

Antifungal Drug Coverage Summary

Neutropenic Enterocolitis (Typhlitis)

Neutropenic Enterocolitis Common Cause of Neutropenic Fever
Mucosal injury → translocation of gut bacteria in neutropenic patients → sepsis

Symptoms

  • Abdominal pain
  • Diarrhea
  • Nausea / vomiting

CT Findings

  • Bowel wall inflammation
  • Bowel wall thickening

Treatment

Piperacillin-tazobactam (Zosyn)
OR
Cefepime + Metronidazole (Flagyl)

⚠️ Also test for C. difficile

💊 Antimicrobial Prophylaxis

⚠️ Indication for Prophylaxis

Antimicrobial prophylaxis is indicated in patients with prolonged neutropenia (ANC <500 cells/µL for ≥7 days). Coverage should include antibacterial, antifungal, and antiviral agents.

Antibacterial Prophylaxis

Agent Target Notes
Levofloxacin Gram-negative organisms including Pseudomonas Standard antibacterial prophylaxis during neutropenia
Ciprofloxacin Gram-negative organisms Alternative; less coverage of gram-positives

🔍 Additional Workup by Symptom

Symptom-Directed Testing

Symptom / Concern Recommended Workup Rationale
Sinus pain or congestion Sinus CT scan Rule out invasive fungal sinusitis (Aspergillus, Mucor)
Concern for intra-abdominal infection CT abdomen/pelvis Identify enterocolitis, abscess, source
Diarrhea C. difficile PCR/GDH Common in immunocompromised patients on antibiotics
Oral lesion Viral culture swab; assess for thrush HSV vs. Candida vs. chemotherapy mucositis
Respiratory symptoms / infiltrates RVP (respiratory viral panel), CXR; consider CT chest Viral vs. bacterial vs. fungal pneumonia
Undiagnosed pulmonary infiltrates Bronchoscopy with BAL Culture BAL for bacteria, fungi, mycobacteria; galactomannan on BAL
AMS, neck stiffness, severe headache LP; Cryptococcal antigen, fungal culture Rule out cryptococcal meningitis

Standard Initial Infectious Workup Checklist

  • Blood cultures ×2 (peripheral or 1 peripheral + 1 from each CVC lumen)
  • Chest X-ray
  • Urinalysis ± urine culture
  • BMP / CMP (renal function, LFTs for antibiotic dosing)
  • CBC with differential (confirm ANC)
  • Assess for localizing symptoms → add directed testing above
  • Review recent culture data and prior organisms for resistance patterns

✓ Bronchoscopy / BAL — When to Consider

Bronchoscopy with bronchoalveolar lavage (BAL) can be used to obtain specimens for culture (bacterial, fungal, mycobacterial) and galactomannan testing when the etiology of pulmonary infiltrates remains unclear despite standard workup.
