📋 New Leukemia Diagnosis

Initial recognition, workup, and urgent overnight management for suspected or confirmed leukemia

🔎 When to Be Concerned About Leukemia

Suspect leukemia when any of the following are present, particularly in combination:

🔺 New severe leukocytosis with no other explanation
🩸 Abnormal bleeding or bruising
📉 New cytopenia
🤒 Frequent infections, fever, chills
🔴 Petechiae
🦷 Gingival hyperplasia
⚖️ Weight loss
🫁 Lymphadenopathy

⚡ Immediate Actions

  1. 1
    Call the Fellow
    Notify the hematology fellow immediately for any new suspected leukemia
  2. 2
    Call the Hematology Lab for Peripheral Smear Review
    Request urgent manual differential and smear review to look for blasts, Auer rods, and morphologic clues
  3. 3
    Obtain Blood Consent
    Consent patient for blood draw, potential bone marrow biopsy, and further procedures

➕ Additional Considerations

⚡ TLS Prophylaxis

  • Aggressive IV fluid hydration
  • Allopurinol (or Rasburicase if high risk)
  • Monitor: K, Phos, Ca, uric acid, Cr, LDH

💊 Antimicrobial Prophylaxis

  • Acyclovir — antiviral (HSV/VZV)
  • Posaconazole — antifungal
  • Levofloxacin — antibacterial

🔗 PICC Line Placement

Place PICC line for reliable IV access — essential for induction chemotherapy, blood draws, and prolonged antibiotics. Discuss timing with the fellow.

🚨 Situations Requiring Urgent Overnight Treatment

WBC >100K — Concern for Leukostasis Emergent

⚠️ Leukostasis is a Medical Emergency

Proceed with cytoreduction while awaiting confirmation of diagnosis. Do not delay treatment for diagnostic workup to complete.

Cytoreduction Options

Agent Notes
Hydroxyurea Most common first-line option — can order a one-time emergency dose overnight (confirm with fellow)
Cytarabine Alternative cytoreduction agent
Leukapheresis Reserve for rapid end-organ damage only; discuss with fellow

Concern for APL (Acute Promyelocytic Leukemia) Do Not Delay

⚠️ APL is Life-Threatening if ATRA is Delayed

Start ATRA empirically if APL is suspected — do not wait for confirmatory molecular testing (PML-RARA).

Signs Suggesting APL

CD34 negative on flow
Auer rods on peripheral smear
DIC / bleeding diathesis
Low WBC with severe coagulopathy
Drug Order As Dose Notes
ATRA (Tretinoin) Search "tretinoin" in order system 22.5 mg/m² — one-time dose Confirm with fellow before ordering overnight
