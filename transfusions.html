☢️ Irradiated Products

⚠️ Always Required for Heme Malignancy Patients

All blood and platelet transfusions for hematology-oncology patients must be irradiated to prevent transfusion-associated graft-versus-host disease (TA-GvHD).

How to Order Always Do This
Select "Heme Malignancy" in the Irradiated Products Dropdown
When ordering any blood product (pRBC or platelets), find the irradiated products dropdown in the order and select "Heme Malignancy".
☢️ Irradiated Products → "Heme Malignancy"
Why irradiate? Irradiation inactivates donor T-lymphocytes in the blood product, preventing them from engrafting and attacking the immunocompromised host's tissues (TA-GvHD). This is particularly dangerous and often fatal in hematology patients.

🧬 Pathogen Reduced Platelets

🧬 What Is Pathogen Reduction?

Pathogen reduction (PR) is a process applied to platelets to inactivate potential pathogens. However, this process can make the platelets themselves less functionally effective, leading to a suboptimal platelet increment after transfusion.

How to Order Non-Pathogen Reduced Platelets Always Do This
Write "Non-Pathogen Reduced" in the Comments Section
Every time you order platelets, add this note in the comments/special instructions section of the order.
💬 Comments: "non-pathogen reduced"

⚠️ Patient Not Responding to Platelet Transfusions?

If a patient is not having the expected platelet increment after transfusions, consider that the blood bank may be issuing "PR PLATELETS" (pathogen reduced) by default.

  • Call the blood bank directly
  • Ask them to add a note to the patient's chart to issue non-pathogen reduced platelets only
  • This can make a significant clinical difference in platelet-refractory patients
