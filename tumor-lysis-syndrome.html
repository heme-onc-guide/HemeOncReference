⚡ Tumor Lysis Syndrome

Recognition, risk stratification, prophylaxis, and treatment of TLS in hematologic malignancies

🔎 When to Be Concerned

⚠️ Highest Risk Window

TLS most commonly occurs when starting treatment — monitor closely during the first 72 hours after chemotherapy is administered.

Also watch for TLS when: A patient is being re-treated at relapse, particularly if they have high-volume disease or previously responded rapidly to therapy.

🧪 Diagnosis

Cairo-Bishop Definition — Laboratory TLS

Requires 2 or more of the following laboratory changes within 3 days before or 7 days after chemotherapy:

Lab Value Threshold Or
Uric Acid >8 mg/dL 25% increase from baseline
Potassium >6 mmol/L 25% increase from baseline
Phosphorus >4.5 mg/dL 25% increase from baseline
Calcium <7 mg/dL 25% decrease from baseline

Clinical TLS Criteria

Laboratory TLS plus one or more of the following:

⚠️ Clinical Criteria

  • Creatinine >1.5× upper limit of normal
  • Seizure
  • Cardiac arrhythmia
  • Sudden death
Key Labs to Monitor
  • Potassium
  • Phosphorus
  • Calcium (ionized)
  • Uric acid
  • BMP (Cr, BUN)
  • LDH

🛡️ Prophylaxis

Risk Stratification

🟢 Low Risk
  • Solid tumors
  • Multiple myeloma
  • CLL
🟡 Intermediate Risk
  • SCLC
  • AML with WBC >25K
🔴 High Risk
  • Lymphomas with large disease burden
  • Leukemia with WBC >100K
  • LDH >2× ULN
  • Renal involvement
  • High baseline uric acid, K, or Phos

Prophylaxis by Risk

Risk Intervention Notes
Low No pharmacologic intervention Monitor labs; hydration as clinically indicated
Intermediate Allopurinol + IV fluids Allopurinol only if uric acid <8 mg/dL; switch to rasburicase if uric acid rises
High Rasburicase + IV fluids Check G6PD before giving rasburicase; always contact fellow
💧 Fluid Hydration Goals for Prophylaxis
  • Target: 2–3 L/m² daily
  • Urine output goal: ≥80–100 mL/m²/hour
  • Use normal saline — avoid lactated Ringer's (contains potassium)

Rasburicase

Rasburicase — Uric Acid Reduction Call Fellow First
Consideration Detail
Indication Uric acid >8 mg/dL
Pre-requisite Check G6PD prior to administration — rasburicase causes hemolysis in G6PD deficiency
Escalation Always contact the fellow before ordering rasburicase

⚠️ G6PD Deficiency Contraindication

Rasburicase is absolutely contraindicated in G6PD deficiency. It can cause severe hemolytic anemia and methemoglobinemia. Confirm G6PD result before administration.

Supportive Care Monitoring

✓ Supportive Care Checklist

  • Telemetry / cardiac monitoring (for arrhythmia from hyperkalemia/hypocalcemia)
  • Strict urine output measurement
  • Frequent TLS labs: uric acid, BMP, phosphorus, ionized calcium
  • Daily weights

🔬 Electrolyte Management

🔺 Hyperkalemia
  • Potassium binders: Lokelma or Kayexalate
  • Calcium gluconate — membrane stabilization
  • Cardiac monitoring (telemetry)
  • Insulin + D50 — shift K intracellularly
🔺 Hyperphosphatemia
  • Phosphate binders:
  • — Calcium acetate
  • — Sevelamer
  • — Calcium carbonate
  • Dietary phosphate restriction
🔻 Hypocalcemia
  • Calcium gluconate IV — only if symptomatic
  • Do not routinely correct asymptomatic hypocalcemia (may precipitate calcium-phosphate crystals)
  • Monitor for tetany, seizure, arrhythmia
🔺 Hyperuricemia
  • Uric acid <8: Allopurinol
  • Uric acid ≥8 or high risk: Rasburicase (check G6PD first)
  • Aggressive IV hydration

🔄 Indications for Hemodialysis

⚠️ Consult Nephrology First

Always consult nephrology before initiating hemodialysis. Severe TLS cases may require HD when medical management fails.

Indication Notes
Refractory Hyperkalemia Not responding to medical management
Oliguria / Anuria Inadequate urine output despite fluids
Fluid Overload Unable to maintain fluid balance with diuretics
Severe Uremia Rapidly rising creatinine or BUN
