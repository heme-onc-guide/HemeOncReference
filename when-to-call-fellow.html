📞 When to Call the Fellow

Escalation guide for overnight and on-call residents in hematology-oncology

💡
Golden Rule: When in doubt, call. Whenever you're feeling unsure about a clinical decision or have a major patient update — call the fellow. There is no penalty for reaching out early.
Specific Situations That Require a Call
🔬
Concern for New Leukemia
New severe leukocytosis, blasts on smear, unexplained cytopenias, or any clinical picture suspicious for a new hematologic malignancy.
📋
New Leukemia Diagnosis
Confirmed new diagnosis — including transfers from an outside hospital presenting with a new leukemia diagnosis. Fellow needs to be aware for workup coordination and urgent treatment decisions.
🧠
Any CRS or ICANS
Not just new presentations — call for any active or evolving CRS/ICANS unless a predefined management plan is already in place.
💉
Consideration of Giving Tocilizumab
Before administering tocilizumab for CRS or ICANS — always confirm with the fellow first regardless of grade.
⚡
TLS with Uric Acid >8 — Consideration of Rasburicase
Rasburicase carries significant risks including hemolysis in G6PD deficiency. Always reach out to the fellow before ordering.
🧪
Chemotherapy Infusion Reactions
Any reaction during a chemotherapy infusion — stop the infusion, stabilize the patient, and call the fellow.
💊 Pharmacy is also helpful in this case
🩸
Blood Product Infusion Reactions
Any reaction to a blood product transfusion — stop the transfusion, send the appropriate reaction workup, and notify the fellow.
🏥 Blood bank is also helpful in this case
⚠️
Any Chemotherapy Delays or Issues
If a scheduled chemotherapy dose needs to be held, delayed, or modified for any reason — labs out of range, access issues, patient refusal, or clinical concerns.
☎️
Still unsure? Just call. It is always better to call and not need to than to not call and wish you had. The fellow is there to support you.
